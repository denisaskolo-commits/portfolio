Denisas Kolomyckis

Interdisciplinary artist working across sculpture, installation and performance, informed by a background in dance and theatre.
Born 1992 (Vilnius, Lithuania) Member: Lithuanian Interdisciplinary Artists’ Association Awards: Young Creator (Ministry of Culture, 2020) • M. K. Čiurlionis scholarship (2021) • ArtVilnius young artist award (2021)

Sculpture & Installation

Selected works & series

Kolomyckis’ sculptural practice frequently merges physical material, staged space and the logic of the body. He completed BA and MA studies in Sculpture at the Vilnius Academy of Arts, following formative training in ballet at the National M. K. Čiurlionis School of Art and studies at the Conservatoire for Dance and Drama in London.

One of the most visible long-term projects, Femina, examines how gender stereotypes shape (and limit) identity and social perception, presented as an installation distributed across public/civic sites in Jonava in collaboration with (AV17) Gallery.

Performance

Body / public space / politics

Performance is central to Kolomyckis’ practice, shaped by years of dance training and stage experience. His work moves between choreographic strategies and sculptural presence—testing how the body can function as a political signal and a social “document” in real time.

His public framing of work connects artistic practice with human rights and social inequality themes, including in the context of recognition as a best young artist at ArtVilnius.
